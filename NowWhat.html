Now What?

As a society, we have gone to great lengths to mechanize and automate much of the core human experience: Robots work our factories, prepare our food, and move us around; AI increasingly writes our news, makes our art, and even our music. Sure, it's great to have a machine handle some of the drudgery of life, but I think that at the end of the day, we might be fucking ourselves a little. So I am making a machine to even take care of that! Now What is a fucking machine, that will only fuck itself. Its power plant shall be an antique hit-or-miss engine (which I already have, and is an appropriately comical and arcane power source for this purpose). It will have a belt drive going to a reciprocating mechanism (a Whitworth 4-bar quick return mechanism; I've landed on this for my design as it produces what I deem to be an acceptably hilarious motion path), on which will be mounted a large, floppy dildo, which will engage with a Fleshlight, also mounted to the machine. A later goal will be to add a closed-loop "lubrication" system, to really j(a/i)zz it up. So great, we have a machine that fucks itself. Now What?

The project will be on two separate wheeled carts that can simply be emplaced, and stowed when complete. Tarps will be laid down to ensure no spillage of anything.

HowMuch

I'd be *very* surprised if there was much, if anything, left over from the default maximum of $575. Much of the framework and linkages can be made from scrap metal already on-hand. I will likely need to purchase several pulleys (to reduce the RPM of the input engine); these will probably be between $20 and $50 each. I will also need to purchase the, ahem, "piston" and "cylinder" components, which seem to be probably $75-$100 each. I can also reasonably assume around $100 worth of assorted components (fasteners, bushings, bearings, belts, etc). I already have the input engine. I am still working to assemble a formal budget, but a quick accounting of the strictly the main, known items (pulleys, jackshaft, piston, and cylinder) total up to about $370.
